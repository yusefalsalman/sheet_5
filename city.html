Amman City

    Amman is the capital city of the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Historically known as "Rabbath Ammon", it was the capital of the ancient Ammonite Kingdom. Later, during the Hellenistic period, it was renamed "Philadelphia" by the Ptolemaic ruler Philadelphus. The city then became part of the Roman and Byzantine Empires.

    Geographic Location:

    Amman is located in north-central Jordan, built on a series of rolling hills between the desert to the east and the fertile Jordan Valley to the west. Its elevation ranges from approximately 750 to 1,100 meters above sea level, giving it a moderate climate.

    Facts About Amman :

  • It is the largest city in Jordan, with a population of over 4 million people.
  • Amman is the political, economic, and cultural center of the country.
  • The city blends modern architecture with ancient ruins, offering a unique urban landscape.
  • Notable landmarks include the Roman Theater, the Citadel (Jabal Al-Qal'a), and various Roman, Byzantine, and Islamic archaeological sites.
